Analog Photography App For iPhone

  • Organize Your Library

    Create folders, sort your rolls, rename or move films with ease. Frames gives you a clean, flexible way to manage your film archive, just like a digital contact sheet built for analog workflows.

  • Capture Every Setting

    Log camera, lens, film stock, exposure, and notes while you shoot. Frames helps you record everything that matters so no detail gets lost between the shutter click and the scan.

  • Revisit With Context

    Review your shot settings anytime—complete with timestamps, notes, and map locations. Easily retrace your steps and connect each frame to the moment it was made.

  • Map Your Memories

    Automatically capture location data for each shot or add it later by hand. View your photos on an interactive map to see exactly where each moment was taken.

  • Export and Share

    Send your notes, settings, and shots to your computer in seconds. Export as .frames files and pick up right where you left off with the macOS app.

Film Photography App For Mac

  • Sync From iPhone

    Seamlessly import your saved film notes from the iOS app. Just drop your .frames file into the macOS app—everything is instantly reloaded, no setup required.

  • Explore Every Shot

    Explore every shot in detail: see your camera settings, film stock, and shooting location on a map. Easily attach your scanned photos to each frame for a complete view.

  • Export With Metadata

    Merge your notes directly into your images. Export your attached photos with full EXIF metadata, including location, lens, and camera info—ready to share or archive.

FAQ

Which photo file formats are supported by the macOS app?

At the moment, the macOS companion app supports JPEG (.jpg) files for metadata embedding and export. TIFF (.tif) support is under consideration and may be added in the future depending on user demand. We’re building around real photographers’ needs, so keep the feedback coming.

Will there be an Android version of the app?

An Android version is something we're thinking about — but for now, our focus is on making the iPhone and macOS experience as polished and complete as possible. If the iOS version proves successful, Android could be next on the roadmap. Stay tuned!

Which kinds of metadata can I record with the app?

You can log essential camera settings like shutter speed, aperture, lens, focal length, and film stock — plus timestamps, GPS location, and even custom notes for each shot. Whether you're capturing landscapes or street scenes, the app ensures your analog photography stays well-documented and organized.

What features are planned for the future?

We’re committed to continuous improvement. Future updates may include translations to make the app more accessible globally, additional export options, and possibly even a watch companion app for quick access during shoots. While nothing is set in stone, we’re building this tool with the community and evolving alongside your workflow.

Can I log things like my tripod setup, filters, or other accessories?

For now, the focus is on keeping the app simple and efficient for most analog photographers. But you're not limited — there's a dedicated Notes field where you can jot down extra info like tripod use, filter types, or any other gear-related details. These aren’t part of the official EXIF metadata specification, but the Notes field gives you the flexibility to include whatever matters to your film workflow, without cluttering the core interface.
